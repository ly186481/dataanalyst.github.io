Customer Data Anlaysis

Customer analytics, also called customer data analysis, is the process of collecting and analyzing customer data to gain insights on customer behavior.

LTV

LTV stands for lifetime value. LTV is a marketing term used to predict the net profit over the entire future relationship with a customer. It can also be more generally defined as the monetary value of a customer based on present value and the projected future value of a customer relationship.

    LTV = (average customer spend) x (number of times the customer is expected to repeat the purchase over one year) x (average length of the relationship, in months or years)

Cohort

Cohort analysis is a type of behavioral analytics in which you take a group of users, and analyze their usage patterns based on their shared traits to better track and understand their actions. A cohort is simply a group of people with shared characteristics.

AARRR

Pirate Metrics framework is an acronym for a set of five user-behavior metrics that product-led growth businesses should be tracking: acquisition, activation, retention, referral, and revenue.

Funnel

ethod of understanding the steps required to reach an outcome on a website and how many users get through each of those steps.

A/B Test

Also known as split testing, refers to a randomized experimentation process wherein two or more versions of a variable (web page, page element, etc.) are shown to different segments of website visitors at the same time to determine which version leaves the maximum impact and drives business metrics.
